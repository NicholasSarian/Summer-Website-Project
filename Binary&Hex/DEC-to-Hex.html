Convert Decimal to Hexadecimal

The easiest method (in my opinion) is converting the decimal to binary then to hex. This is a two step process. Let's use the number 150. Big number, eh? Don't worry, it's not all that bad. First, convert 150 to binary. 150 in binary is 10010110. Remember to split the number so you'll have 1001 0110. 1001 is 9 and 0110 is 6. The hexadecimal number of 150 is 96. Assuming you've read the other two pages first, this is very simple.

Convert Hexadecimal to Decimal

Converting the hexadecimal back to decimal is as simple as reversing the above process. 96 gets split up to become 9 and 6. 9 becomes 1001 and 6 becomes 0110. Together, that's 10010110. The binary string 10010110, converted into decimal, becomes 150. At this point, you're just repeating everything you've learn previously. Converting between decimal, binary, and hexadecimal is very easy to understand. You just got to get your head into it and you'll get it.
