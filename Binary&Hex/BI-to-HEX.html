Convert Binary to Hexadecimal

Before we start, let's use the binary string 10010111 in this example to learn how to convert it. Converting this is hexadecimal is actually super easy. First, you want to split the string into 2 sets of 4 bits which would look like this 1001 0111. Assuming you've read the first page first before going here, you know how to convert binary to decimal. These 2 sets of 4 bits are treated separately and therefore, the count (from the farthest right bit) starts with 1. 1001 becomes 9 and 0111 becomes 7. Now you combine the two numbers to get 97. The hexadecimal for 10010111 is 97.

Convert Hexadecimal to Binary

Converting hexadecimal to binary is just as easy, if not easier. You got the hex number 97. All you do is separate the two numbers and convert each to binary then conbine them again. Basically, you're reversing the process above. 9 becomes 1001 and 7 becomes 0111. Combine them to get 10010111. That's it! Seriously, that's it, it's very simple.
