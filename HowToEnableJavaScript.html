How to Enable JavaScript

Here's how you can enable JavaScript in Chrome, Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox, Opera, and Safari:

Google Chrome

  1. In the upper right hand corner of the window, click on the three dot menu.
  2. Go down and click on "Settings".
  3. Click on the three bar menu icon in the upper left corner of the window, expand "Advanced", and click on "Privacy and security".
  4. Scroll down until you find "Content settings" then click on it.
  5. Scroll down and find "JavaScript". Click on it and click on the slider to enable JavaScript.
  6. Refresh the page

Internet Explorer

  1. Click on the gear icon in the upper right corner then click on "Internet Options".
  2. Go to the "Security" tab.
  3. On the "Security" tab, click on the "Custom level..." button.
  4. In the "Secuirty Settings - Internet Zone" window, scroll down (near the bottom of the page) until you find the "Scripting" section.
  5. Find "Active Scripting" and enable it then click "OK".
  6. Click "Yes" on the warning window then "OK" to close the window.
  7. Refresh the page

Mozilla Firefox

  1. In the upper right corner, click on the three bar menu icon then click on "Options".
  2. Select the "Content" section from the options at the top of the pop-up page.
  3. Check the box next to "Enable JavaScript" and click "OK".
  4. Refresh the page

Opera

  1. Click on the Opera Button at the top-left corner of the window and click on "Settings".
  2. Click on the "Websites" item in the left window pane.
  3. Under JavaScript, select the "Allow all sites to run JavaScript (recommended)" option.
  4. Now close the "Settings" window and restart Opera.

Apple Safari

  1. Open Safari then click on "Safari" in the top left of your screen and choose "Preferences".
  2. Click on the "Security" tab.
  3. Beside "Enable JavaScript", if the box is checked, JavaScript is enabled. If it's not checked, JavaScript is disabled. Check this box to enable JavaScript.
  4. Close the "Preferences" window and refresh the page.
